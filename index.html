Ross Goodwin is a data poet, an artist, creative technologist, hacker, gonzo data scientist, and a former White House ghostwriter. Goodwin employs machine learning, natural language processing (NLP) & generation (NLG), physical computing, and other technologies to realize new forms and interfaces for writing.

Goodwin’s projects and collaborations have earned international attention: from word.camera, a camera that expressively narrates its photographs in real time using artificial neural networks (web app + installations, 2015-present), to SUNSPRING, the world's first film created from an AI-written screenplay (2016 short film, directed by Oscar Sharp, starring Thomas Middleditch); from making London’s Trafalgar Square lions roar poetry (2018, Please Feed The Lions with Es Devlin), to writing a novel with a car (2017-2018, 1 the Road), to co-creating the lyrics for the Grammy-nominated album Chain Tripping by YACHT.

Working outside traditional arts funding models, Goodwin has received sponsorship from large technology companies like Google and AT&T, media companies like iHeart and BuzzFeed, news outlets like The Economist and Univision, academic institutions like Teacher’s College at Columbia University, and fashion houses like Rag & Bone. However, Goodwin also has produced significant volumes of indepedent creative work directly from his personal studio: prototyping original ideas rapidly on limited budgets, and sharing results in real time both online and physically.

Although he began his B.S. degree in Physics in 2005-2006, and despite working full-time on the 2008 Obama campaign and 2008-2009 Obama-Biden Presidential Transition Team as a ghostwriter and completing numerous other internships along with extracurricular commitments, Goodwin earned his undergraduate degree in Economics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in May 2009. His first job after graduation was a Presidential Writer position at The White House, where he wrote nearly 100 Presidential Proclamations. After next working at the U.S. Treasury Department on financial regulatory reform, Goodwin learned computer code, then gravitated immediately to natural language processing, natural language generation, and computational creative writing. He earned his graduate degree from the Interactive Telecommunications Program a.k.a. ITP at New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts in May 2016.

Winner of the 2018 IDFA DocLab Award for Digital Storytelling as well as Columbia University's Digital Dozen Breakthroughs in Storytelling Award for 2019, Goodwin has exhibited work &/or spoken at Ars Electronica, London Design Festival, NYC Fashion Week, Sundance Film Festival, Cannes Lions, SXSW, the Barbican, the London Science Museum, the V&A Museum, the Serpentine Galleries, Science Gallery International, the World Economic Forum, the TriBeCa Film Festival Interactive Showcase, the International Center of Photography, AT&T Shape, TEDxBoston, MLconf, IDFA DocLab, Vivid Sydney, Phi Centre, Gray Area, the MIT Media Lab, Columbia University Teacher’s College, Zurich University of the Arts, Maker Faire, Future of Storytelling, GitHub Universe, Strange Loop, GOTO Chicago, Series Mania, the NeurIPS machine learning conference, STATE Festival, Retune Festival, Molasses Books, and other venues.

P.P.S. Here is a list of questions that have fascinated Ross since 2014, presented in no particular order:

  • What does it mean to be “literate” in today’s world?

  • Has changing the opposition’s vocabulary &/or redefining their terms to create negative connotations become a common tactic in political communication today? If so, why? For the US, could it be said that Republicans & Democrats no longer speak the same language on economic issues? If so, what societal harm(s) will/have these tactics cause(d)? & if they have, how could such harm(s) be reversed? & is there a way to get opposing political parties to use the same vocabulary in order to promote mutual understanding & policy awareness among the public?

  • Using a uniform set of rules to divide sentences & paragraphs into string data, how can we visualize different forms of writing to illuminate their inherent syntactic / metasyntactic structures in the clearest possible way?

  • Is it possible to program a computer to write poetry or literature in english? What about entertaining works of fiction?

  • Which economic policy decisions include the greatest number of decision-making participants? Which decisions include the least? What transformation, if any, does the relative number of participants in an economic policy decision have on the relative scale of its impact, or on the probability of a successful outcome?

  • What can Advanced Chess teach us about the nature of human intelligence? From studying advanced chess games, would it be possible to draw conclusions about specific factors that computers may need to achieve human-like intelligence?

  • What measurable effect(s) does the physical presence of a player have on a game of chess? How could such effect(s), if present, be measured? Could measuring such effect(s), if present, involve building a modern version of The Turk? &, for such a device, how would deviating from “The Turk” as an aesthetic model for the dummy alter game outcomes? &, given a state of altered outcomes, what would be the plausible implications? Also, would it be possible to build a modern version of The Turk that could play blitz chess on a normal chess board against human opponents?

  • What design &/or technology features could be added to today’s standalone & smartphone-integrated image sensors to improve various use cases? /li>
  • How could a car-sharing model be designed for daily commuters in suburbs/exurbs of major cities that lack adequate public transit? Would it be feasible to integrate a variety of locally-optimized energy solutions into such a model to maximize externality reductions?

  • The moment a commercial airline flight takes off, isn’t it true that each empty seat becomes a useless waste of resources, representing a loss of potential profits for the airline company, a loss of potential mobility for member(s) of the public, & wasted energy? If so, how could that situation be rectified for the good of all involved (or potentially-involved) parties?

  • Should the U.S. Government become paperless? If so, how (and how soon) could that be accomplished? Also, how (if at all) do the previous two questions relate to this one: To what (if any) extent should governments in democratic countries remain inefficient-by-design in order to prevent tyranny? (& if they should remain inefficient to any extent, to what extent will that sacrifice affect operating capacity &/or probable outcomes in a political, economic, domestic, &/or foreign relations crisis?)

  • Is Bd3 ever a good move for white in certain lines of the Sicilian Alapin Variation? (e.g. 1. e4 c5 2. c3 Nf6 3. e5 Nd5 4. Bd3 … 5. Bc2 …)

  • Are any of these sonnets any good?

  • What aspects, if any, do all the questions above share?
